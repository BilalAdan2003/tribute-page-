Dr wangari mathai

THE WOMAN WHO WON THE NOBEL PRICE

Here is a time line of Dr wangari mathai's life:

  • 1940-Born in ihithe, KENYA
  • 1943-Maathai's family relocated to a White-owned farm in the Rift Valley, near the town of Nakuru, where her father had found work
  • 1947-she returned to Ihithe with her mother, as two of her brothers were attending primary school in the village
  • 1950finished studying high school at loreto high school in limuru
  • 1960- passed the exams and Maathai became one of some 300 Kenyans selected to study in the United States
  • 1966Maathai received her MSc in biological sciences,[18] and was appointed to a position as research assistant to a professor of zoology at University College of Nairobi.[19]
  • 1977- Maathai founded the Green Belt Movement,[2][3] an environmental non-governmental organization focused on the planting of trees, environmental conservation, and women's rights.
  • 1984-she was awarded the Right Livelihood Award for "converting the Kenyan ecological debate into mass action for reforestation".
  • 2003-2005-Maathai was an elected member of the Parliament of Kenya
  • 2005-served as Assistant Minister for Environment and Natural Resources in the government of President Mwai Kibaki
